## Character Identity Lock

Gu Huaizhang is Yan Nanzhi's southern teacher, an older Zhaoming remnant official-scholar, and a Fuyizhe political pressure figure. He should read as ceremonially controlled, intelligent, and heavy with preserved old-imperial authority. His threat is not combat; it is etiquette, memory, doctrine, and the demand that Yan Nanzhi become the Sun-Moon banner.

Age and body:

- older adult, approximately 60 to 68
- slim, upright, carefully held posture
- long scholar-official hands, controlled teaching gestures
- silver-streaked black hair, neat topknot or tied back hair, restrained beard or moustache
- refined but severe face, sunken scholar cheeks, attentive eyes, no monstrous or mystical anatomy
- movement suited to scrolls, ritual instruction, private orders, and court memory rather than battlefield action

Core visual anchors:

- southern red-line scrolls and red cord knots
- old-imperial robe fragments preserved as living clothing, not museum scraps
- lightweight vermilion gauze outer robe, royal blue or peacock-blue inner layer, moon-white underlayer
- worn-gold woven edges and small court hardware
- vine-green southern plant dye accents and damp-proof dark-brown travel layer
- breathable wide sleeves, short outer cover, layered but humid-climate practical construction
- oil-paper wrapped scrolls, red-line wooden scroll tube, damp-proof lacquer case
- partial Zhaoming sun-moon marks only as inherited embroidery, seal pressure, or worn pendant detail
- old white-stone, cracked lacquer screen, damp archive table, and hidden southern hall context

## State Notes

### Southern Edict State

This is Gu Huaizhang as Yan Nanzhi's living political teacher. Use a light vermilion outer robe over a royal-blue inner layer, with a short breathable over-jacket and worn-gold edge control. The red-line scroll bag is the most important prop. The costume should be complete, expensive, and adapted to humid southern survival. It is not a palace costume untouched by history and not a refugee rag.

### Old-Imperial Ritual Teaching State

This is the strongest pressure-memory state. Make the posture frontal, upright, and ceremonial. Use Ming-inspired formal Hanfu structure, wide sleeves, moon-white underlayer, woven-gold route/cloud/star marks, and controlled vertical drape. The Zhaoming sun-moon system may appear only as dark inherited embroidery or a worn-gold partial pendant, not as a new chest emblem. The image should make Yan Nanzhi's burden understandable without turning Gu into a simple villain.

### Secret-Letter Travel State

This state compresses the same noble costume into a covert, damp-proof messenger shape. Gathered sleeves, darker short overcoat, oil-paper scroll tube, red-line wooden cylinder, and lacquer case must be clear. He remains an old-court teacher carrying orders, not an assassin, martial spy, modern diplomat, or fantasy wizard.
