## Character Identity Lock

Meng Guizang is an adult Old Book Society survivor and dead-book archivist, visually aged 35 to 45. He provides archive clues at the Ashen Academy and carries the physical memory of book burning, mechanical prohibition, Qingming identifier training, and falsified imperial history. He must read as a precise, wary archival survivor with intact professional structure, not a ragged beggar, wandering immortal, court scholar, priest, or battlefield commander.

Core visual anchors:

- half of one eyebrow singed away by an old fire
- ash marks on face and hands, localized and readable
- deep grey scholar travel robe with scorched edges but intact silhouette
- smoke-black book-box harness over the body
- blackened portable book box with charred corners and cord lashings
- chest paper clips, bone-fragment page clips, wax-sealed small pouches, old book cords
- bone slips holding burned residual pages
- paper-yellow, bone-white, smoke-black, char-brown, and tiny old-vermilion stamp palette
- narrow protective hand posture around fragments and records
- no open clean emblem, no new Old Book Society logo, no readable writing

## State Notes

### Ashen Academy Guide State

Use the Ashen Academy palette: charcoal black, book ash, cracked copper, grey-white dust, collapsed-roof cold light, and very small moon-white star-map glints. Meng Guizang's robe is old and scorched, but the garment remains complete, fitted, and professional. The book box is a functional archive carrier, not a fantasy backpack or treasure chest.

### Archive-Reveal State

This state makes his plot function legible. He protects fragments, not power. The opened book box should reveal layered paper packets, bone clips, wax pouches, charred wood dividers, old cords, and a few partial seal traces. No page may contain readable text. Any Zhaoming or Qingming symbol language must be broken, partial, and attached to evidence rather than to Meng Guizang's identity.

### Marsh/Flight Archive-Guard State

This state carries his background as a swamp dead-book survivor. Add dark reed-fiber overwrap, damp ash on the lower robe, tightened cords, and a wrapped book box. He remains clean enough to read as a careful archivist, not a mud-covered vagrant. The overwrap should not hide the book-box harness, bone clips, or half-burned eyebrow.
